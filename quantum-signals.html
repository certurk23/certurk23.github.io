What Is Quantum Signals?

Quantum Signals is a post-close multi-factor technical signal engine designed for US equities. Every trading day after market close, the system scans 30 pre-selected large-cap stocks across multiple sectors and applies six independent technical indicators to each ticker.

When 4 or more indicators simultaneously confirm a bullish setup, the stock receives a BUY signal. Stocks with fewer than 4 confirmations are marked as WATCH — meaning they show partial technical interest but lack sufficient confluence for a high-conviction entry.

The engine is fully rules-based with zero discretionary override. Every parameter is fixed and transparent.

Decision Logic: The 4+ Confluence Rule

The core principle behind Quantum Signals is multi-factor confluence. No single indicator is reliable enough on its own. The system requires agreement from at least 4 out of 6 independent technical dimensions before generating a BUY signal.

decision_logic.pyPython
def generate_decision(signals_count, total_indicators=6):
    """
    signals_count: number of bullish indicators (0-6)
    Returns: 'BUY' if 4+ signals confirm, else 'WATCH'
    """
    if signals_count >= 4:
        return "BUY"
    return "WATCH"

Tier Classification

  • Tier 1 (6/6 signals): Maximum confluence — all six indicators align. Historically the highest-conviction setup.
  • Tier 2 (5/6 signals): Very strong confluence — one indicator dissenting. Still a high-quality signal.
  • Tier 3 (4/6 signals): Minimum threshold for BUY — the system's entry boundary. Valid but lower conviction.
  • WATCH (0-3/6): Insufficient confluence. On radar but no actionable signal.

The Six Signals

Each indicator independently evaluates one technical dimension. They are intentionally diverse to avoid correlation clustering:

1. RSI (Relative Strength Index) — Momentum

The 14-period RSI measures momentum oscillation between 0–100. The system flags a bullish signal when RSI is between 30–50, indicating oversold-to-neutral recovery — the zone where mean-reversion entries historically perform best.

2. MACD Crossover — Trend Momentum

The classic MACD (12, 26, 9) generates a bullish signal when the MACD line crosses above the signal line. The system checks for crossovers within the last 3 trading sessions to capture fresh momentum shifts.

3. Bollinger Band Position — Volatility & Mean Reversion

Using 20-period Bollinger Bands (2 standard deviations), the system generates a bullish signal when the current price is at or below the lower band — a classic mean-reversion setup.

4. Volume Confirmation — Institutional Activity

A bullish signal fires when volume exceeds 1.2x (120%) of the 20-day average, indicating above-normal institutional participation. Volume confirmation separates genuine moves from low-conviction noise.

5. Price vs. 50-Day SMA — Intermediate Trend

A bullish signal triggers when the current price crosses above or is trading above the 50-day SMA, confirming the stock is in an intermediate uptrend or transitioning from a downtrend.

6. Price vs. 200-Day SMA — Long-Term Trend

When price is above the 200-day SMA, the stock is in a secular bull trend. This filter prevents the system from generating BUY signals in stocks that are in structural decline.

Dashboard Column Definitions

The Quantum Signals dashboard displays 12 data columns for each scanned stock:

Decision

BUY or WATCH. The final output of the 4+ confluence rule.

Tier

For BUY signals only: Tier 1 (6/6), Tier 2 (5/6), or Tier 3 (4/6). Higher tiers historically correlate with better outcomes.

Symbol

The stock ticker (e.g., AAPL, MSFT, NVDA). All symbols are US-listed large-cap equities.

Price

The closing price as of the scan date. Used as the reference point for all calculations.

RSI(14)

The current 14-period Relative Strength Index value. Values in the 30–50 zone are flagged as bullish.

Signals

A count showing how many of the 6 indicators are currently bullish (e.g., “5/6”).

Ref. Entry

A reference entry price calculated as the average of the lower Bollinger Band and the 50-day SMA. Educational context only.

Stop

A reference stop-loss level set at 3% below the reference entry price. Not a recommendation.

Target

A reference target price calculated using a 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio from the reference entry. Educational only.

Hold Days

The suggested hold period from backtest optimization, typically 5–15 trading days.

WR% (Win Rate)

The historical win rate of similar signal configurations from backtesting. Subject to survivorship and lookahead limitations.

BT Return% (Backtest Return)

The average return percentage from backtested trades with the same signal tier. Gross return before commissions and slippage.

Important Interpretation Notes

Backtest ≠ Future Performance

All WR% and BT Return% values are derived from historical backtesting. Real-world execution differs due to slippage, commissions, liquidity, gap risk, and market regime changes.

  • BUY ≠ “Buy immediately”: A BUY signal means the technical setup meets the system's confluence threshold. It is not a trade recommendation.
  • WATCH = Partial setup: Stocks with 1–3 signals may be approaching a BUY signal in coming sessions.
  • Tier matters: Tier 1 signals (6/6) historically show stronger forward returns than Tier 3 (4/6).
  • Volume is the validator: Volume confirmation often separates profitable setups from false signals.
  • Combine with fundamentals: Quantum Signals is purely technical. Earnings dates, FDA decisions, and other catalysts can override any technical setup.
  • No system is infallible: Even 6/6 Tier 1 signals can fail. Position sizing and stop-loss discipline are critical.
