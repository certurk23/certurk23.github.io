What Is Quantum Signals?

Quantum Signals is a post-close multi-factor technical signal engine designed for US equities. Every trading day after market close, the system scans 500+ stocks across all sectors and applies 93 independent technical indicators to each ticker using data from Twelve Data API.

When the optimized confluence threshold is met (currently 4+ signals), the stock receives a BUY signal. Stocks below the threshold are marked as HOLD. Stocks are ranked into tiers (S, A, B, C) based on historical backtest performance.

The engine is fully rules-based with zero discretionary override. All parameters are optimized via genetic algorithm and validated out-of-sample.

Decision Logic: Multi-Factor Confluence

The core principle behind Quantum Signals is multi-factor confluence. No single indicator is reliable enough on its own. The system evaluates 93 independent signals per stock and requires a minimum confluence threshold (optimized via genetic algorithm) before generating a BUY decision.

decision_logic.pyPython
def generate_decision(signals_count, confluence_threshold=4):
    """
    signals_count: number of bullish indicators (0-93)
    confluence_threshold: optimized via genetic algorithm
    Returns: 'BUY' if threshold met, else 'HOLD'
    """
    if signals_count >= confluence_threshold:
        return "BUY"
    return "HOLD"

Tier Classification

Stocks are ranked into tiers based on historical backtest performance (Sharpe ratio, win rate, total return):

  • ★ S-Tier: Elite performers — highest Sharpe ratio, best risk-adjusted returns in backtesting. Highest conviction.
  • A-Tier: Strong performers — above-average backtest metrics. High conviction.
  • B-Tier: Solid performers — acceptable backtest metrics but with wider variance.
  • C-Tier: Emerging signals — limited trade history or lower conviction metrics.
  • HOLD: Below confluence threshold. Not displayed in the dashboard.

The 93-Indicator Signal Architecture

The engine evaluates 93 independent technical indicators across 7 categories. Each indicator produces a binary signal (1 = bullish, 0 = not). Key categories include:

Momentum Oscillators

RSI (multiple periods: 7, 14, 21), Stochastic RSI, Williams %R, CCI, MFI, and custom momentum divergence detectors. The system identifies oversold recovery zones across multiple timeframes simultaneously.

Trend & Moving Averages

SMA/EMA crossovers (multiple pairs: 9/21, 20/50, 50/200), ADX trend strength, Supertrend, VWAP position, and multi-period trend alignment. Price vs. 50-day and 200-day SMA serve as intermediate and long-term trend filters.

Volatility & Mean Reversion

Bollinger Bands (position, squeeze, bandwidth), ATR-based volatility regime detection, Keltner Channel interactions, and Donchian Channel breakouts. The system identifies statistically cheap entries relative to recent trading ranges.

Volume Analysis

Volume ratio vs. 20-day average, OBV trend, volume-price divergence, VWAP deviation, and accumulation/distribution patterns. Volume confirmation separates institutional moves from noise.

Pattern Recognition

Candlestick pattern detection (engulfing, hammer, doji), support/resistance level interactions, and price action confirmation signals.

Macro & Relative Strength

Sector relative strength vs. SPY, beta-adjusted momentum, and cross-asset correlation signals for regime detection.

Statistical Filters

Z-score normalization, percentile rank signals, and multi-period autocorrelation filters to identify mean-reverting vs. trending conditions.

Dashboard Column Definitions

The Quantum Signals dashboard displays 12 data columns for each BUY signal:

Decision

BUY only. HOLD signals are hidden from the dashboard. BUY means the stock has met or exceeded the confluence threshold.

Tier

S-Tier (elite), A-Tier (strong), B-Tier (solid), C-Tier (emerging). Based on historical backtest Sharpe ratio, win rate, and cumulative return.

Symbol

The stock ticker (e.g., AAPL, MSFT, NVDA). All symbols are US-listed large-cap equities.

Price

The closing price as of the scan date. Used as the reference point for all calculations.

RSI(14)

The current 14-period Relative Strength Index value. Values in the 30–50 zone are flagged as bullish.

Signals

A count showing how many of the 93 indicators are currently bullish (e.g., “21/93”).

Entry

The reference entry price — current closing price at signal generation. Educational context only.

Stop

A reference stop-loss level set at 14% below entry (GA-optimized parameter). Not a recommendation.

Target

A reference target price at 7.5% above entry (GA-optimized parameter). Educational only.

WR% (Win Rate)

The historical win rate for this specific stock from backtesting. How often the stock hit its target before the stop or hold period expired.

BT Return% (Backtest Return)

The cumulative backtest return for this stock using the signal system. Gross return before commissions and slippage.

Trades

Total number of historical trades executed for this stock in backtesting. Higher counts indicate more statistical significance.

Important Interpretation Notes

Backtest ≠ Future Performance

All WR% and BT Return% values are derived from historical backtesting. Real-world execution differs due to slippage, commissions, liquidity, gap risk, and market regime changes.

  • BUY ≠ “Buy immediately”: A BUY signal means the technical setup meets the system's confluence threshold. It is not a trade recommendation.
  • HOLD = Below threshold: Stocks not meeting the confluence requirement are hidden from the dashboard entirely.
  • Tier matters: S-Tier signals historically show stronger risk-adjusted returns than C-Tier. Use tier as a conviction filter.
  • Volume is the validator: Volume confirmation often separates profitable setups from false signals.
  • Combine with fundamentals: Quantum Signals is purely technical. Earnings dates, FDA decisions, and other catalysts can override any technical setup.
  • No system is infallible: Even 6/6 Tier 1 signals can fail. Position sizing and stop-loss discipline are critical.
